Brand Story

Doctium represents healthcare that is available without friction. The identity combines a medical cross, a D-shaped care loop, and a clean technical wordmark to communicate clinical reliability, continuous access, and engineered product quality.

Trust

Deep navy anchors the brand in clinical seriousness, privacy, and secure patient data handling.

Access

The open circular mark suggests care that is reachable, connected, and always moving with the patient.

Precision

Geometric forms and disciplined spacing align the brand with high-performance software engineering.

Logo Concept

The logomark is built from two ideas: a medical cross for care and a structured D form for Doctium. The light-blue cross softens the experience for patients, while the navy D gives the platform a secure, enterprise-ready presence.

The logo should never feel decorative. In product surfaces it functions as a trust signal: calm, precise, readable, and separated from clinical alerts or patient data.

Usage Rules

Approved

  • Use the full signature for brand introductions and marketing pages.
  • Use the isolated mark for app icons, favicons, launchers, and tight UI spaces.
  • Keep the navy and sky-blue pairing intact for official brand use.
  • Place the logo on clean, high-contrast backgrounds.
  • Keep the tagline only when it remains clearly legible.

Not Allowed

  • Do not stretch, skew, rotate, crop, or redraw the symbol.
  • Do not recolor the logo with warning, error, or success colors.
  • Do not add shadows, bevels, glows, gradients, or glass effects.
  • Do not place the logo over patient imagery or busy EHR data surfaces.
  • Do not recreate the wordmark with a standard typed font.

Clear Space and Minimum Size

Define X as the width of the vertical stem in the light-blue medical cross. Maintain at least 1X of clear space around the full logo and 0.75X around the isolated app mark. In dense clinical dashboards, no alerts, patient identifiers, filters, or appointment chips should enter this space.

UI Voice

Product Language

Clear, calm, direct, and clinically careful. Avoid hype in patient-care flows and reserve urgency for true action states.

Marketing Language

Confident and accessible. Emphasize anytime access, trusted clinicians, secure records, and continuity of care.

Final Brand Principles

Reliable

Every visual choice should support trust in care delivery, identity, and patient data handling.

Efficient

Interfaces should feel fast, readable, and task-focused for both patients and providers.

Human

Clinical precision should be balanced with warm, accessible language and patient-friendly hierarchy.

Product Expression

Patient Experience

Patient-facing screens should reduce anxiety: simple actions, clear appointment status, visible provider context, and calm confirmation states. Use sky blue and teal as supportive cues, not decorative noise.

Provider Experience

Provider-facing workflows should feel dense but ordered. Use navy for structure, neutrals for data-heavy content, and status colors only where they clarify clinical or operational priority.
